Italy

about

Italy’s history rolls back millennia. As soon as you step on Italian soil, you will spot its ancient history and culture everywhere. Historic Italian cities such as Rome will show you gigantic traces of a mighty ancient empire, the Papal kingdom, and Renaissance noble families, while smaller cities like Florence will take you through the Middle Ages. You’ll also find Middle Age imprints in the towns of Viterbo, Bracciano, Perugia, or Venice. Sardinia, on the other hand, is home to the Nuraghe stone towers from the Bronze Age, and villages belonging to the Mediterranean island’s ancient civilization.

Rome

Rome, the city of seven hills, enjoyed a mythic beginning. Romulus and Remus – twin brothers who were nursed by a she-wolf and fathered by a war god – reportedly founded the Eternal City. And although historians are a little skeptical about this epic entry into the world, most travelers are absolutely certain that there is something magical about Rome. Whether it's the mystery of nearby Vatican City or the ghosts of the Colosseum, an afternoon caffè on Piazza Navona or a piled-high plate of pasta at a trattoria, Rome is sure to enchant.


Italy'captal city, Rome is also known for a history that dates back to the eras of Octavian, Julius Caesar and Hadrian, among others. Left behind are structures like the Pantheon, the Roman Forum and dozens of churches, among other historic gems. Art enthusiasts will relish the trove of art housed at the Vatican Museums, and foodies will enjoy the splendid Italian fare, not to mention the gelato. And though its momentous past is the focus for many vacationers, Rome is also a fast-paced, modern and relevant city, with gleaming designer storefronts, sleek hotels and cutting-edge restaurants.

Attractions in Rome

The Colosseum

Rome's #1 attraction that no tourist dares to miss. It is recommended to get tickets in advance to save the queues.

The Vatican Museum

The pinnacle of art and architecture in one place. The Vatican Museums are simply not to be missed. A unique and unforgettable tourist experience!

The pantheon

The Pantheon is one of the most impressive buildings in Rome. Most travelers visit it through a professional guided tour.

Borghese Gallery

amazingly beautiful art gallery located within the wonderful Borghese Gardens. Must see without a doubt. Book tickets in advance to reserve a convenient date.

Florence

The city of Florence lies on the banks of the Arno River and is surrounded by the charming landscapes of the beautiful Tuscany region, which is its capital. It is one of the most beautiful cities in Italy and Europe as a whole and throughout history the biggest names in the world of culture have lived there - Michelangelo, Dante and Machiavelli. Florence is the cradle of the Renaissance, with a rich cultural and artistic past and present, which are milestones in world history, therefore, it is a must-stop in Italy for every tourist, and especially for culture and art lovers. The Uffizi Gallery is Florence's most famous museum and displays important works from the Renaissance, including Botticelli's famous work, "The Birth of Venus". Michelangelo's famous statue, David, stands in the Accademia Gallery in Florence and apart from these, there are other museums scattered around the city, which curate works of art, whose value is priceless.


Attractions in Florence

Piazzale Michelangelo

Piazzale Michelangelo is a square in Florence, on a hill on the south bank of the Arno river, from which you can see the view of the historic center of Florence and all its main attractions. In the center of the square, on a raised platform, there is a replica of Michelangelo's statue, David.

The Piazza della Signoria

The Piazza della Signoria (Signoria Square) is Florence’s main square. It is located between the Piazza del Duomo and the Arno River

Ponte di Rialto

The beautiful bridge was designed by an artist named Antonio and he prevailed in the center of design over big names like Palladio and even Michelangelo, in his own right. On the bridge today there are many souvenir and jewelry shops and near the bridge there is a Rialto market.

Palazzo Pitti

Palazzo Pitti is mainly known for having been the residence of the Medici family. Today, it houses important Florentine museums, not to mention its marvelous Boboli Gardens.
